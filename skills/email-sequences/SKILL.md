# Email Sequences

Write high-converting email sequences using proven direct-response frameworks. This skill covers 4 flow types, each with a specific purpose and structure.

## Quick Reference: Flow Types

| Flow | Purpose | Emails | When to Use |
|------|---------|--------|-------------|
| Lead Magnet Delivery | Deliver resource, build trust | 2 | Just signed up for freebie |
| Welcome/Nurture | Build relationship, demonstrate expertise | 5 | After delivery, before selling |
| Sales Conversion | Present offer, handle objections | 6 | Ready to pitch |
| Educational Email Course | Daily education, build authority | 6 | Standalone lead magnet |

**To write a specific flow**, read the relevant reference file in `references/`.

## Gathering Context

Before writing, you need to understand the business. If the user hasn't provided these details, ask for them upfront — one message, not a series of questions.

Each flow type has its own Required Inputs table in its reference file. Load the relevant reference file first, then use that table as your checklist.

**General variables to identify across all flows:**

| Variable | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| `{Audience}` | Specific target market (e.g., "first-time homebuyers in Texas", not "people") |
| `{Desire}` | Ultimate goal they want to achieve |
| `{Problem}` | The specific painful problem being solved |
| `{Offer}` | Product or service being sold (name, what it is, price if relevant) |
| `{Unique Mechanism}` | What makes their approach different from others |
| `{Current Belief}` | The limiting belief holding their audience back |
| `{End Belief}` | The new perspective you want them to hold |
| `{Objections}` | 2-3 barriers to buying, with answers if known |
| `{Testimonials}` | Real client success stories (Before → After → How) |
| `{Sender Name}` | Who signs the emails |

**Not all variables are needed for every flow.** Check the reference file for the specific inputs required.

**How to ask:** Combine into one message, e.g.:
> "To write great emails for you, I need a few things: Who is your audience? What are you selling and what transformation does it promise? What objections do people typically have? Any real client success stories? Who should sign the emails?"

## Core Philosophy

### Never Fabricate Content

**Critical:** Never make up testimonials, case studies, statistics, client names, or specific results. If required content is missing:

1. **Ask for it** — Request the specific testimonial, case study, or data point needed
2. **Mark as placeholder** — If proceeding without it, clearly mark the section:
   ```
   > ⚠️ PLACEHOLDER — REPLACE WITH REAL TESTIMONIAL
   > This is an example story. Please replace with a real customer story.
   ```
3. **Work around it** — Restructure the email to avoid the missing element (e.g., use a paradigm shift instead of a case study)

This applies to:
- Client success stories and testimonials
- Specific results, numbers, or timeframes
- Named individuals (even first names)
- Statistics or research claims
- Quotes attributed to real people

**When in doubt, ask.** It's better to pause and request real content than to fabricate something that damages trust.

---

### Customer is the Hero

Position the customer as the protagonist. You (the brand) are the **guide** who helps them succeed. Every email should focus on their transformation, not your features.

### The 1=1 Rule

**1 Email = 1 Core Idea.** Don't overload. If you have multiple points, write multiple emails. Each email has one clear purpose.

### Belief Shifting

Persuasion is belief change. Move readers from their **Current Belief** (limiting) to an **End Belief** (empowering, aligned with the offer). Structure sequences to shift beliefs progressively.

### Value Before Ask

The nurture flow earns the right to pitch. Follow the **80/20 rule**: 80% value, 20% soft brand-building. Only sell after trust is established.

## Email Writing Rules

Apply these rules to every email:

### Format
- **NO initial greetings** (no "Hi {name}") — start with the hook
- **NO emojis** — unless explicitly requested
- **4th-grade reading level** — simple words, clear sentences
- **Short paragraphs** — 2-3 sentences max, plenty of white space
- **200-350 words** per email (scannable, mobile-friendly)
- **Sign off** with the sender name at the end

### Tone
- Conversational — write like talking to one friend
- Focus on "You" (the reader)
- Be direct — don't apologize for selling
- Empathetic but authoritative

### Structure
- **One clear CTA** per email
- End with a **teaser/open loop** for the next email
- Use subject line strategies: outcome-focused, curiosity, urgency

### Quality Checklist
Before finalizing any email:
- [ ] 1 Email = 1 Core Idea?
- [ ] No greeting at the start?
- [ ] No emojis?
- [ ] 4th-grade reading level?
- [ ] Under 350 words?
- [ ] Clear CTA?
- [ ] Teaser for next email?

## Flow 1: Lead Magnet Delivery

**Purpose:** Deliver what was promised. Build initial trust. Do NOT sell.

**Read:** [references/lead-magnet.md](references/lead-magnet.md)

**Quick Structure:**
1. **Email 1 (Immediate):** Thank them, deliver the asset, set expectations
2. **Email 2 (Day 1-2):** Consumption nudge, quick-win tip, engagement prompt

**Key Rule:** No sales pitch, no brand history. Just deliver value.

---

## Flow 2: Welcome/Nurture

**Purpose:** Build relationship, demonstrate expertise, position as authority. 80% value, 20% soft brand-building.

**Read:** [references/welcome-nurture.md](references/welcome-nurture.md)

**Quick Structure:**
1. **Brand Introduction** — Origin story, why you help people like them
2. **Name Their Big Problem** — PAS framework (Problem-Agitation-Solution)
3. **Quick Win** — One actionable tip (5-10 min to implement)
4. **Social Proof** — Client success story (Before → After → How)
5. **Soft Bridge to Offer** — Value → Gap → Bridge

**Timing:** Every 2-3 days, Days 3-14

---

## Flow 3: Sales Conversion

**Purpose:** Present offer, handle objections, drive action.

**Read:** [references/sales-conversion.md](references/sales-conversion.md)

**Quick Structure:**
1. **Introduce Offer** — Problem → Guide → Solution
2. **Case Study** — Before → Transformation → After
3. **Overcome Objection #1** — Acknowledge → Reframe → Prove
4. **Overcome Objection #2** — "Will this work for ME?"
5. **Paradigm Shift** — Old Way → New Way → Vision
6. **Urgency + Final CTA** — Recap → Stakes → Action

**Timing:** Every 1-2 days, after nurture flow

---

## Flow 4: Educational Email Course (EEC)

**Purpose:** Daily education over 5 days. Builds authority, grows list. Can soft-pitch on Day 5.

**Read:** [references/eec.md](references/eec.md)

**4 Format Options:**

| Format | Best For | Structure |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Crash Course | Beginners needing foundation | Day 1: Key concepts, Day 2: Mistakes, Day 3: Steps, Day 4: Upgrades, Day 5: Best practices |
| Step-by-Step | Clear process to outcome | Each day = one step, reader does something tangible |
| Biggest Mistakes | Avoiding costly errors | Each day = one mistake + how to solve |
| Easy Templates | Plug-and-play resources | Each day = one template + example |

**Structure:** Day 0 (intro + outline) + Days 1-5 (content)

## Frameworks Reference

### PAS (Problem-Agitation-Solution)
1. **Problem:** State it clearly
2. **Agitation:** Emotional/practical toll
3. **Solution:** Reframe (not product pitch yet)

### Before → After → How
1. **Before:** Their situation/struggle
2. **After:** The transformation/result
3. **How:** What they did (your method)

### Old Way → New Way → Vision
1. **Old Way:** How most people think (why it keeps them stuck)
2. **New Way:** The shift that changes everything
3. **Vision:** What's possible after the shift

### Value → Gap → Bridge
1. **Value:** What they've learned
2. **Gap:** What's still missing
3. **Bridge:** How your offer fills the gap

## Subject Line Strategies

**Lead Magnet:**
- "Here's your {Lead Magnet Name}"
- "Your free {resource} is inside"

**Nurture:**
- "The #1 mistake I see..."
- "Quick win for {Audience}"

**Sales:**
- "How {Client} got {specific result}"
- "Are you ready for {transformation}?"
- "Last chance: {offer} closes tonight"

**EEC:**
- "Day 2: The mistake that costs most {audience} $X"
- "Day 3: Step 3 — This is where it gets real"

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Pitching too early** — Sales before trust kills conversions
2. **Information overload** — One point per email
3. **Generic tone** — Write like a real person
4. **Ignoring mobile** — Short paragraphs, simple formatting
5. **Weak CTAs** — Be direct about what you want them to do
6. **No open loops** — Always tease the next email
