# Welcome/Nurture Flow

**5 emails | Days 3-14 (every 2-3 days) | 80% value, 20% brand-building**

## Purpose

Introduce your brand, build trust, provide ongoing value, and position yourself as an authority — without making a hard sales pitch. Follow the 80/20 rule.

---

## Required Inputs

Before writing, gather:

| Input | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Sender name | Who signs the emails |
| Origin story | 2-3 sentences: where you started, what changed, why you help |
| Audience label | Specific target market (2-4 words) |
| Avatar brief | 2-3 sentence description of one ideal person |
| Pain points | 3 specific, acute problems (1 sentence each) |
| Quick-win tip | One actionable tip (5-10 min to implement) |
| Client success story | Before/after transformation |
| Offer teaser | Brief description of paid offering |
| Brand voice | (optional) Tone modifications |

---

## Email 1: Brand Introduction

**Goal:** Build personal connection

### Framework: Origin Story
1. Where you started
2. What changed
3. Why you help people like them now

### Must Include:
- Your story and mission (use origin story)
- Why you do what you do
- Establish empathy with their problem
- Be personal and relatable

### Template

```
[Hook — something relatable to their situation]

[Origin story — where you started, what you struggled with]

[The turning point — what changed everything]

[Why you do this now — mission, empathy]

[Tease what's coming in future emails]

{Sender Name}
```

---

## Email 2: Name Their Big Problem

**Goal:** Show you deeply understand their pain

### Framework: PAS (Problem-Agitation-Solution)
1. **Problem:** State it clearly (use first pain point)
2. **Agitation:** Describe the emotional/practical toll
3. **Solution:** Reframe how to think about it (NOT a product pitch)

### Must Include:
- Describe the core problem they face
- Agitate the emotional impact
- Offer a perspective shift

### Template

```
[Hook — state the problem with a rhetorical question]

[Agitation — why this problem sucks, the toll it takes]

[Stakes — why it's important to solve now]

[Perspective shift — reframe how to think about it]

[Tease the quick win coming next]

{Sender Name}
```

---

## Email 3: Quick Win / Value

**Goal:** Build credibility through a small, immediate result

### Framework: Single Tactical Win
1. The problem this solves
2. The exact steps (keep it simple)
3. What they should see/feel after

### Must Include:
- One actionable tip they can implement in 5-10 minutes
- Clear, specific instructions
- Expected outcome

### Template

```
[Hook — promise a quick result]

Here's something you can do in the next 10 minutes:

[Step 1]
[Step 2]
[Step 3]

When you're done, you should [expected outcome].

[Engagement prompt — ask them to try it]

{Sender Name}
```

---

## Email 4: Social Proof / Story

**Goal:** Demonstrate real-world results

### Framework: Before → After → How
1. **Before:** Their situation/struggle
2. **After:** The transformation/result
3. **How:** What they did (your method, without being salesy)

### Must Include:
- One detailed client/customer story
- Specific, tangible outcomes (numbers when possible)
- Reader should see themselves in the story

### Template

```
[Hook — introduce the client or situation]

**Before:**
[Where they were, the struggle]

**After:**
[The transformation, specific results]

**What made the difference:**
[Your method/approach, without pitching]

[Reflection — question for reader]

{Sender Name}
```

---

## Email 5: Soft Bridge to Offer

**Goal:** Transition naturally toward your solution

### Framework: Value → Gap → Bridge
1. **Value:** Recap what they've learned
2. **Gap:** What's still missing or hard to do alone
3. **Bridge:** How your offer fills that gap

### Must Include:
- Connect the value provided to your paid offering
- Frame product/service as the logical next step
- Soft CTA (not hard sell)

### Template

```
[Hook — reference the value you've delivered]

Over the past few emails, you've learned:
- [Quick recap point 1]
- [Quick recap point 2]

But there's a gap:
[What's still hard or missing]

That's exactly why I created {Offer}.

[Brief description of what it is and the transformation]

[Soft CTA — "If you're interested..." or "When you're ready..."]

{Sender Name}
```

---

## Key Rules

1. **Conversational tone** — write like you're talking to one person
2. **One email = one point** — don't overload
3. **~300 words per email** — scannable, mobile-friendly
4. **End each email with a teaser** — open loop for the next
5. **No hard selling** until Email 5 (and even then, soft)

---

## Business Type Adaptations

| Business Type | Email 2 (Problem) | Email 3 (Quick Win) | Email 4 (Proof) |
|---------------|-------------------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Coaches | Common mindset block or strategic mistake | "The 5-minute fix for..." | Client transformation with specific outcome |
| Local Services | Why DIY fails or what most providers miss | Seasonal maintenance tip | Before/after with photos or metrics |
| Course Creators | The knowledge gap holding them back | Mini-lesson or framework preview | Student success story with timeline |
| Professional Services | Compliance risk or money on table | Quick checklist or self-assessment | Case study (protected details) |
