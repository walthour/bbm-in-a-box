# Educational Email Course (EEC)

**6 emails | Day 0 + Days 1-5 (daily) | Education that builds authority**

## Purpose

A 5-day email course that educates subscribers on a specific topic while building authority and trust. EECs are powerful lead magnets themselves — people exchange their email for valuable education delivered over 5 days.

---

## Choose Your Format

There are 4 types of EEC. Choose the one that fits your content:

| Format | Best For | Daily Structure |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| **Crash Course** | Beginners who need a foundation | Key concepts → Mistakes → Steps → Upgrades → Best practices |
| **Step-by-Step** | Clear process to outcome | Each day = one step (reader does something tangible) |
| **Biggest Mistakes** | Avoiding costly errors | Each day = one mistake + how to solve it |
| **Easy Templates** | Plug-and-play resources | Each day = one template + example |

---

## Required Inputs

Before writing, gather:

| Input | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Format | Crash Course, Step-by-Step, Biggest Mistakes, or Easy Templates |
| Topic | What the course teaches |
| Target audience | Specific, not generic (e.g., "college students" not "people") |
| The 5 items | 5 concepts/steps/mistakes/templates depending on format |
| Negative outcomes | What bad things happen if they don't learn this (2-3, tangible) |
| Sender name | Who signs the emails |
| Credibility | Brief intro on why you're qualified to teach this |
| Course name | (optional) Tangible-sounding name (Roadmap, Blueprint, etc.) |
| Objection whisper | (optional) "Even if..." or "And how to..." |
| Day 5 CTA | (optional) Paid offer to soft-pitch at the end |
| Brand voice | (optional) Tone modifications |

## Building "The Offer" (Course Name)

Before generating, build a compelling headline:

### Step 1: Core Statement
- Crash Course: "Everything You Need To Know About {Topic}"
- Step-by-Step: "How To {Achieve Outcome} In 5 Steps"
- Biggest Mistakes: "Biggest Mistakes {People} Make Doing {X}"
- Easy Templates: "{Number} {Type} Templates For {Outcome}"

### Step 2: Get Specific on Audience
- Bad: "Biggest Mistakes People Make Fundraising"
- Good: "Biggest Mistakes College Students Make Fundraising For Their First Startup"

### Step 3: Add Negative Outcomes
**Tangible > Intangible:**
- Intangible: "Nobody takes you seriously"
- Tangible: "Investors leave you on 'read'"

### Step 4: Give It a Name
Use words that feel physical:
- Roadmap, Blueprint, Checklist, Accelerator, Kickstart Guide, Cheat Sheet

### Step 5: Add the "Whisper"
Overcome the main objection:
- "(And How To Solve Them)"
- "(Even If You've Never Done This Before)"
- "(Without Spending a Dime on Ads)"

### Final Formula
**[Name]: [Core Statement] That Lead To [Negative Outcomes] ([Whisper])**

**Example:**
"The College Entrepreneur's Roadmap: Avoid These 5 Fundraising Mistakes That Lead To Investors Leaving You On 'Read' And $0 Raised (Even If You've Watched 100 Hours Of Shark Tank)"

## Universal Structure

### Day 0: Introduction & Course Outline (Immediate)

**Goal:** Welcome them, set expectations, build anticipation

**Must Include:**
- Thank them for signing up
- Brief intro to you and your credibility
- What they'll learn over the next 5 days (outline)
- What outcome they can expect by Day 5
- Teaser for Day 1

**Tone:** Warm, excited, confident

**Subject:** "Welcome! Here's your 5-day {Course Name}"

### Days 1-5: Content Emails (Daily)

**Goal:** Deliver one piece of valuable education per day

**Must Include:**
- Day's topic clearly stated
- Actionable content (varies by format — see below)
- Teaser for next day (except Day 5)

**Day 5 additions:**
- Recap of full course
- Soft CTA to paid offer (if applicable)

---

## Format-Specific Structures

### Crash Course

**Day 1: Most Important Things To Know**
- 3-5 key concepts with brief explanations
- Actionable takeaway

**Day 2: Common Mistakes**
- 3-5 mistakes with why they're harmful
- How to avoid each

**Day 3: Steps To Get Started**
- 3-5 steps to go from zero to one
- What "done" looks like

**Day 4: Simple Upgrades**
- 3-5 optimizations to accelerate
- Quick wins

**Day 5: Best Practices**
- 3-5 practices for ongoing success
- Course recap

---

### Step-by-Step

Each day = one step

**For each day, include:**
- The step clearly stated
- Why this step matters
- Exactly how to do it (specific instructions)
- What "done" looks like

**Day 5:**
- Final step
- Recap of all 5 steps
- What they've accomplished

---

### Biggest Mistakes

Each day = one mistake

**For each day, include:**
- The mistake clearly stated
- Why people make this mistake
- The negative outcome if not fixed
- How to solve it

**Day 5:**
- Final mistake
- Recap of all 5 mistakes
- What they now know to avoid

---

### Easy Templates

Each day = one template

**For each day, include:**
- Template name
- Problem it solves
- How to use it (step-by-step)
- Real example showing it in action

**Day 5:**
- Final template
- Recap of all 5 templates
- How to use them together

---

## Key Rules

1. **Daily sends for Days 1-5** — tight cadence keeps momentum
2. **Each email is standalone valuable** — not just a teaser
3. **End every email with an open loop** — teaser for next day
4. **Day 5 can soft-pitch** — natural next step
5. **300-500 words per email** — dense but scannable
6. **Use formatting** — numbered lists, bullets, bold text

## Subject Line Strategies

**Day 0:**
- "Welcome! Here's your 5-day {Course Name}"
- "Your {Course Name} starts now"

**Days 1-5:**
- "Day 2: The mistake that costs most {audience} $X"
- "Day 3: Step 3 — This is where it gets real"
- "Day 4: Template #4 — My most-requested one"
- "Day 5: Final lesson + what's next"
