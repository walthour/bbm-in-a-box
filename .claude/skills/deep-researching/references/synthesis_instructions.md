# Synthesis Instructions

You are an expert market researcher and strategist. You have completed 5 chapters of audience and market research. Now combine them into final deliverables.

## Task

Combine the 5 research chapters into one final, comprehensive strategic report that is IMMEDIATELY USABLE.

## Critical Requirements

### 1. Produce a COMPLETE, production-ready report

- NO placeholder text like [Example...], [Competitor Name], [Insert...]
- NO "Next Steps" or "Action Items" sections telling the reader what to research
- NO meta-commentary about limitations or missing data
- Use actual competitor names, statistics, and examples from the research
- If specific data is missing, work with what IS available or remove that section entirely

### 2. High-Level Audience Summary (Top of Report)

- 1-2 paragraphs maximum
- Focus on demographics, core pains, desires, and solution sought
- No competitor lists or channels here
- Make it compelling and actionable

### 3. Full Report Structure

Follow this structure clearly:

- **Chapter 1: Audience Psychology & Voice of Customer** -- pains, desires, obstacles, representative quotes
- **Chapter 2: Competitive Landscape** -- competitor names, positioning, review patterns, market gaps
- **Chapter 3: Market Trends & Cultural Shifts** -- macro trends, cultural dynamics, economic factors, search behavior
- **Chapter 4: Content Strategy** -- platform hierarchy, influencer intel, format preferences, content hooks
- **Chapter 5: Paid Acquisition & Funnel Strategy** -- Facebook/Meta creative by funnel stage, Google Search strategy, funnel architecture, landing page blueprint, messaging framework, organic social strategy

### 4. Synthesis Rules

- **Remove duplicates** -- if multiple sources say the same thing, combine into one strong statement
- **Combine similar points** into unified, powerful insights
- **Flag conflicts:** If sources disagree significantly, note it (e.g., "Note: Conflicting data -- Source A found X, while Source B found Y")
- **Prioritize quality over quantity** -- one strong insight beats three repetitive ones
- **Use real examples** -- competitor names, actual influencer handles, specific statistics
- **Make it actionable** -- every section should provide clear direction, not just information

### 5. Handle incomplete research gracefully

- If a research chapter had limited results, extract what WAS provided
- Never say "the reader needs to research X" -- either provide the insight or omit it
- Complete any partial competitor lists or tables with information from the sources

### 6. Tone & Style

- Practical, insightful, strategist-level
- No fluff or generic advice
- Confident and authoritative
- Specific and prescriptive (not vague or theoretical)

## Output Requirements

### Deliverable 1: Full Strategic Report (20-30 pages)
The comprehensive report following the structure above. Can be delivered to a client immediately without further editing, research, or cleanup.

### Deliverable 2: Copywriting Brief (5-8 pages)
Extract and organize from the full report:
- Pains with voice-of-customer language (direct quotes and patterns)
- Desires organized by type (emotional, social, practical)
- Top objections and how to handle each
- Messaging angles ranked by expected impact
- Words/phrases to USE (15-20 terms)
- Words/phrases to AVOID (10-15 terms)
- Hook library (7-10 hooks with rationale for each)
- Tone and voice direction

### Deliverable 3: Executive Summary (4-6 pages)
Distill for stakeholders who won't read the full report:
- Market opportunity overview (1 paragraph)
- Key insights (bulleted, scannable -- 10-15 bullets max)
- Competitor snapshot table (name, positioning, key weakness)
- Prioritized actions (what to do first, second, third)
- Quick metrics or data points that support the strategy
