# Chapter 2: Competitive Landscape (Local & National)

**MISSION:** Map the competitive environment by identifying key players, their positioning strategies, and where they're failing to meet the target audience's needs.

**RESEARCH AREAS:**

## 1. Direct Competitors

- Search for top competitors in the product/service category described in the audience brief
- If a location is specified, focus on local/regional competitors in that area
- If national/online, focus on major national players and digital competitors
- Identify 8-12 major players (flexibility based on market size)
- For EACH competitor, include:
  - Name and location (if applicable)
  - **Website URL** (mandatory)
  - **Instagram handle/URL** (if prominent - especially for consumer brands)
  - Other prominent social media (LinkedIn for B2B, TikTok for Gen Z brands, etc.)
  - General offerings/services
- Note which ones appear most frequently in search results and have significant online presence

## 2. Positioning Patterns

- Analyze how competitors position themselves (websites, social media, Google business descriptions, LinkedIn)
- Identify POSITIONING CATEGORIES relevant to this industry (examples: Budget/Value, Premium/Luxury, Tech-Forward, Traditional, Full-Service, Specialized, etc.)
- For pricing: include if readily available on websites, but don't worry if it's not public
- Note the TONE and LANGUAGE they use: casual vs professional, disruptive vs traditional, technical vs accessible, etc.
- Identify what customer segment each competitor seems to target

## 3. Review Analysis - Pattern Identification

- Review customer feedback on relevant platforms (Google Reviews, Yelp, G2, Capterra, Amazon, Trustpilot - depends on industry)
- Identify PATTERNS in complaints (not exhaustive lists):
  - Common complaint themes relevant to this category
  - For each pattern, provide 1-2 representative quote examples
- Also note positive review patterns (what are competitors doing well?)
- Look for recurring language customers use when describing problems or praise

## 4. Copy & Creative Trends

- Analyze the messaging, visual styles, and content patterns competitors are using
- Common hooks and angles being used (e.g., fear-based, aspirational, problem-solution, social proof)
- Visual style trends (minimalist, bold, clinical, lifestyle, before/after heavy, etc.)
- Content patterns on social media and websites
- Identify what's oversaturated vs. what's underutilized
- Note opportunities to differentiate creatively

## 5. Market Gaps & Opportunities

- Based on complaint patterns and positioning analysis, identify unmet needs
- What does everyone else do that the target audience dislikes?
- Where is there white space for the positioning described in the audience brief?
- Be specific about the opportunity, not just generic statements
- Tie gaps back to the audience's pains and desires from their brief

## Search Strategy

Based on the audience brief, construct searches like:
- "best [product/service category]" + [location if provided]
- "[product/service]" + [location] + reviews
- "[competitor name]" + reviews site:yelp.com OR site:google.com OR site:g2.com OR site:trustpilot.com
- "[product/service category]" + [location] - look at top 10-15 results
- "[competitor name]" site:[competitor-website.com] - to analyze their messaging

**Review platforms by industry type:**
- B2B/SaaS: G2, Capterra, TrustRadius
- Local Services: Google Maps, Yelp, Angi
- Products: Amazon reviews, Reddit product discussions
- SaaS: G2, Capterra, ProductHunt

## Important Instructions

- Create a COMPLETE competitive overview with real competitor names and insights
- **Use a TABLE FORMAT** for the competitor overview section with columns: Competitor Name | Website | Instagram | Positioning | Strengths | Weaknesses | Pricing (if available)
- Don't leave placeholder text like "[Example Company]" - use actual businesses you find
- **Include clickable URLs** for all competitor websites and social media profiles
- If you can't find specific information (like pricing), explicitly state "pricing not publicly available" rather than leaving blanks
- Organize into clear categories: direct competitors table, positioning analysis, review insights, copy/creative trends, market gaps
- The competitive landscape should be immediately actionable, not a list of things the user needs to research
- Adapt your analysis to the specific industry and business model (B2B vs B2C, local vs national, product vs service, etc.)

**OUTPUT GOAL:** A comprehensive competitive map that clearly identifies who the players are, how they position themselves, where they're failing, and what opportunity exists for differentiation based on the target audience's specific needs.
