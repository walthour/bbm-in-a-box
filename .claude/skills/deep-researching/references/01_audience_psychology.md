# Chapter 1: Audience Psychology & Voice of Customer

**MISSION:** Conduct deep research into the psychological profile of the target audience described in the audience brief. Through web search, pattern analysis, and synthesis, identify recurring themes in how they speak, what they fear, and what they desire.

**RESEARCH AREAS:**

## 1. Pains & Frustrations

- Search for discussions related to the product/service category and audience described
- Identify PATTERNS in complaints, fears, and frustrations
- For each major pattern you identify, provide ONE representative example/quote that captures it
- Look for emotional language: "I'm exhausted," "I hate," "I'm worried about," "I'm scared of," "frustrated by"
- Search relevant forums, Reddit, Quora, review sites based on the industry/category

## 2. Desires & Aspirations

- What do they want emotionally and practically?
- Focus on "Social & Status" drivers (how they want to be perceived) and "Internal Confidence" drivers (how they want to feel)
- Identify patterns in aspirational language: "I want to," "I hope," "My goal is," "I wish I could"
- Provide representative examples for each major desire pattern

## 3. Obstacles & Objections

- What makes them skeptical or hesitant about solutions in this category?
- Identify trust-barriers, price concerns, fear of bad outcomes, past negative experiences
- Search for review patterns mentioning doubts, hesitations, or negative experiences
- Provide representative examples of common objections

## 4. Current Solutions & Why They Fall Short

- What are they using now to solve this problem?
- What existing solutions (competitors, alternatives, workarounds) are they trying?
- Why are current solutions failing them? What gaps exist?
- Search for discussions comparing solutions, reviews mentioning disappointment, or "looking for alternatives" posts
- Provide examples of common complaints about existing solutions

## 5. Voice of Customer Language Patterns

- Capture the actual phrases and terminology this audience uses
- When you find a strong representative quote, include it with context AND link when possible (e.g., "Reddit discussion in r/[relevant-sub]" with URL, or "Yelp review" with link)
- If exact quotes aren't available, synthesize the common language patterns you observe
- Include source links for forums, Reddit threads, or reviews where you found insights

## Search Strategy

Based on the audience brief provided, construct searches like:
- "[product/service category]" + problem/pain keywords + site:reddit.com OR site:quora.com
- "[product/service]" + "nervous" OR "worried" OR "scared" OR "frustrated" + relevant sites
- "[location if provided]" + "[category]" + reviews site:yelp.com OR site:google.com
- "[category]" + "trust" OR "recommendations" OR "advice" + forums
- Related product reviews on Amazon/relevant review sites (for language patterns of this demographic)
- "[demographic descriptor]" + "[category]" + "experience" OR "advice"

**Adapt by audience type:**
- B2B: LinkedIn discussions, industry forums, G2/Capterra reviews
- Consumer: Reddit, Facebook groups, Amazon reviews, Yelp, Google reviews
- Local: Add location modifiers to searches
- Specific age/demo: Include demographic terms in searches

## Important Instructions

- Focus on PATTERNS over exhaustive quote collection
- One strong representative example per pattern is better than 10 similar quotes
- If you can't find specific forum posts, use available search results to identify themes and synthesize insights
- Be explicit about what you found vs. what you're inferring
- Adapt your search strategy based on the specific audience, location, and category provided

**OUTPUT GOAL:** 4-5 pages of detailed audience intelligence organized by patterns, with representative examples that capture how this specific audience actually speaks and thinks.
