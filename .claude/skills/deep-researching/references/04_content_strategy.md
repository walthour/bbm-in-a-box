# Chapter 4: Content Strategy & Consumption Habits

**MISSION:** Determine how to capture and hold the target audience's attention. Identify where they consume content, what formats resonate, and what messaging approaches work based on the audience described in the brief.

**RESEARCH AREAS:**

## 1. Platform Hierarchy & Usage Patterns

- Research where the specific demographic (from audience brief) spends their time online
- Compare platform preferences relevant to this audience: Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, TikTok, YouTube, Email/Newsletters, Forums, Blogs, Podcasts, etc.
- Search for: "[demographic descriptor] social media usage", "[age/income/profession] platform preferences", "[industry] audience digital behavior"
- Look for engagement data by platform and demographic
- Determine PRIMARY platform (most time/attention) vs SECONDARY vs TERTIARY platforms
- Consider B2B vs B2C differences (e.g., B2B may favor LinkedIn, industry forums; B2C may favor Instagram, TikTok)

## 2. Creator/Influencer Ecosystem

- Identify who this demographic follows for advice/inspiration in this category
- Search for top creators, thought leaders, experts in this specific industry/niche
- If location-specific, include local influencers and thought leaders
- For EACH influencer/creator identified, include:
  - Name and handle (e.g., @username)
  - **Primary platform URL** (Instagram, LinkedIn, YouTube, TikTok - whichever is most relevant)
  - Follower count (approximate if exact number unavailable)
  - Engagement rate or style (if observable)
  - Brief description of their content style
- Provide 5-8 specific names/handles with links that represent different tiers (micro to macro influencers, or niche experts to broad authorities)
- Examples: Industry experts, educators, practitioners, lifestyle influencers adjacent to the category

## 3. Content Format Preferences

- What formats does this specific audience engage with most?
- Analyze: Short-form video (Reels/TikTok) vs Long-form video (YouTube) vs Static imagery vs Text-based content (blogs, newsletters, whitepapers) vs Audio (podcasts)
- Do they prefer educational content, aspirational content, entertainment, data-driven, or case study formats?
- Look at engagement patterns on competitor accounts and similar businesses in this category
- Consider audience sophistication level (technical audiences may prefer depth; busy executives may prefer quick insights)

## 4. Content Hooks & Messaging Approaches

- Identify what types of hooks and angles work for this specific audience
- Categories to explore (adapt based on audience psychology):
  - Fear-based: "mistakes to avoid", "warning signs", "what not to do"
  - Aspirational: "achieve [desired outcome]", success stories, transformation
  - Educational: "how it works", "expert explains", data and insights
  - Social proof: testimonials, case studies, "results our clients love"
  - Problem-solution: "struggling with X? here's how to solve it"
  - Contrarian: "why everyone is wrong about X"
- Which approach resonates most with this audience based on their psychology (skeptical? aspirational? analytical?)?
- Find examples of successful content from competitors or adjacent industries

## Search Strategy

Based on the audience brief, construct searches like:
- "[demographic] social media behavior" OR "digital habits"
- "[industry/category] influencers" OR "thought leaders"
- "[industry] content marketing" + "what works"
- Top-performing accounts in this category (analyze engagement)
- Relevant hashtags and trending topics in this space
- "[audience demographic] + [platform] + behavior" studies
- "[industry] marketing case studies"

**Platform research by audience type:**
- B2B professionals: LinkedIn, industry forums, trade publications, webinars
- Consumers: Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, YouTube, blogs
- Technical audiences: Reddit, Stack Exchange, specialized forums, GitHub
- Local services: Google, Facebook, NextDoor, local review sites
- Gen Z: TikTok, Instagram, YouTube Shorts
- Millennials: Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, Podcasts
- Gen X/Boomers: Facebook, Email, YouTube

## Important Instructions

- Provide SPECIFIC recommendations, not generic statements
- **Include real URLs and links**: actual influencer profiles with URLs, successful content examples with links, specific platform links
- Include real examples: actual influencer handles with profile URLs, successful content types with example links where possible
- Create a clear platform hierarchy (e.g., "Primary: Instagram, Secondary: Email newsletters, Tertiary: LinkedIn")
- Identify 3-5 specific content angles/hooks with examples (link to examples when possible)
- Make this actionable for a creative team to immediately implement
- Adapt recommendations based on the specific audience demographic, psychographic, and behavioral patterns from the brief

**OUTPUT GOAL:** 4-5 pages of actionable content strategy including platform recommendations, influencer benchmarks, format preferences, and proven messaging approaches tailored to this specific audience. This should serve as a technical blueprint for content creation.
